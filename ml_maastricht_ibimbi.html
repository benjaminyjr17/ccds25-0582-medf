Motivation Letter

MSc in International Business: Information Management and Business Intelligence, Maastricht University
To the Admissions Committee,
School of Business and Economics
Maastricht University

My undergraduate education in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence at Nanyang Technological University has provided a strong quantitative foundation, confirmed by an 88th percentile score in the quantitative section of the GMAT Focus Edition (595 overall) and an IELTS Academic score of 8.5. My coursework and applied projects have demonstrated that consequential business problems require structured economic reasoning and managerial frameworks that a computing degree alone does not provide. This recognition directs my application to the MSc in International Business: Information Management and Business Intelligence, which I regard as an academically necessary and intellectually deliberate step toward building the specialized knowledge required to bridge the gap between technical data analysis and strategic business management.

I have actively sought to bridge my technical training with managerial principles by completing minors in Business (Banking and Finance Specialization) and Communication Studies, accumulating 60 ECTS across 10 business modules. Courses in Corporate Finance (A−), Marketing for the 21st Century (A−) and Fundamentals of Management (A+) introduced functional business frameworks, while advanced modules in Alternative Investments (A) and Consumer Insights (A−) required applying analytical reasoning to unstructured problems such as asset class evaluation and quantitative survey design. These results confirm my aptitude for business disciplines but also reveal the limits of what elective coursework can achieve without a systematic grounding in the principles of information management and business intelligence.

My academic projects reinforced this assessment. I developed a complete valuation model and investment committee memorandum for a leveraged buyout of Nilörngruppen AB, a Swedish labeling company, which required financial modeling under uncertainty and structured data management across multiple valuation scenarios. I structured a quantitative consumer behavior study of over 200 respondents for Oatside and conducted a brand audit for Starbucks across international markets, both of which demanded translating data into actionable business intelligence for managerial decision-making. My final-year project, developing an ethical decision framework for AI systems, confirmed that effective governance of technology requires a deeper understanding of how organizations manage information and make decisions than my technical training has provided. Each of these projects exposed the gap between possessing analytical capability and applying it within the systematic information management frameworks that drive organizational success.

To transition from my technical background to a strategic management role, I recognize the need to build a systematic and rigorous foundation in information management and business intelligence. My goal is to complement my project-based experience in finance and marketing with the structured theoretical knowledge that a rigorous master’s curriculum assumes. The MSc in International Business: Information Management and Business Intelligence at Maastricht University is my deliberate choice to acquire this comprehensive grounding. Its curriculum, with courses in Data Management (EBC4091), Cases in Management Information Systems (EBC4038), Business Process Management (EBC4059), and Business Intelligence Case Studies (EBC4107), offers the methodical instruction in applying IT and business intelligence techniques to address complex business challenges that I require to completely capitalize on the advanced, research-driven coursework of the MSc. The School of Business and Economics’ triple-crown accreditation (AACSB, EQUIS, AMBA), placing it among the top one percent of business schools worldwide, and its Problem-Based Learning approach, which develops the critical thinking, teamwork, and problem-solving skills that this specialization demands, align precisely with my objective of working at the intersection of data analytics and corporate strategy within an international business context. Maastricht’s position at the crossroads of European business, with direct industry links to firms such as DSM, Vodafone, and KLM, further reinforces the program’s capacity to prepare graduates for leadership roles in information management and business intelligence.

My application is driven by the recognition that a career in information management and business intelligence demands a correct and complete intellectual foundation. My quantitative record and applied project experience indicate my readiness for this rigorous training. I am prepared to commit completely to this essential stage of my academic development and welcome the opportunity to discuss my application.
